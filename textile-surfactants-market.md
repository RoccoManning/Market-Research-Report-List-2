<p><p>Textile surfactants are chemicals used in the textile industry for various processes like dyeing, washing, and finishing. There are four types of textile surfactants: anionic, cationic, non-ionic, and amphoteric/zwitterionic. Anionic surfactants have a negative charge and are commonly used as wetting agents. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge and are used as antistatic agents. Non-ionic surfactants have no charge and are versatile, used for emulsification and stain removal. Amphoteric surfactants have both positive and negative charges, making them suitable for cleaning, foaming, and conditioning applications. These surfactants play a crucial role in textile manufacturing processes.</p></p>

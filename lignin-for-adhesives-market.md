<p><p>Lignin is a complex organic compound found in plants that provides structural support. In the adhesives market, there are three types of lignin used as ingredients: lignosulfonates, Kraft lignin, and others. Lignosulfonates are obtained from the sulfite pulping process and are commonly used as dispersing agents in adhesives. Kraft lignin is derived from the Kraft pulping process and is utilized as a binder in various adhesives. The "others" category refers to lignin sourced from alternative processes or specific plant species that can be used for adhesive applications.</p></p>

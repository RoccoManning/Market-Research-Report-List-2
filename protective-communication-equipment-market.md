<p><p>Protective communication equipment refers to devices used to safeguard individuals from hazardous environments. There are two main types: passive and intelligent. Passive equipment, such as earplugs or earmuffs, provides a physical barrier to reduce noise or protect against impact. Intelligent equipment, on the other hand, utilizes advanced technology to actively monitor and respond to environmental conditions. This can include devices that automatically adjust noise cancellation or provide real-time alerts for dangerous situations. Both types aim to ensure the safety and well-being of individuals working in high-risk environments.</p></p>

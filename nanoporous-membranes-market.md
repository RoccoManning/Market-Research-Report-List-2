<p><p>Nanoporous membranes refer to a type of filter-like structure with tiny pores on a nanoscale level. They can be classified into two main market types: organic nanoporous membranes and inorganic nanoporous membranes. Organic nanoporous membranes are made from organic polymers and offer advantages like flexibility and tunability. In contrast, inorganic nanoporous membranes are constructed from materials like metals, ceramics, or glasses, providing enhanced durability and thermal stability. Both types serve diverse applications in various industries, including water purification, gas separation, and biomolecule filtration.</p></p>

<p><p>Diagonal tires, also known as bias-ply or cross-ply tires, are a type of tire construction where the cords in the tire body run at an angle to the direction of travel. These tires have a layered design with cords placed diagonally across the tire from bead to bead, providing strength and stability. Diagonal tires are commonly used in a variety of vehicles, including cars, trucks, motorcycles, and agricultural vehicles.</p><p>The market analysis of the diagonal tire industry suggests a positive outlook in the coming years. The market is expected to witness substantial growth at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 14.6% during the forecast period. Several factors contribute to the market growth, including increasing vehicle production, rising demand for commercial vehicles, and expanding automotive aftermarket.</p><p>One significant trend in the diagonal tire market is the increasing preference for radial tires over diagonal tires. Radial tires provide better fuel efficiency, improved traction, and enhanced stability compared to their diagonal counterparts. However, diagonal tires still hold a significant share in the market due to their affordability, strength, and durability. Moreover, these tires are widely used in off-road applications and heavy-duty vehicles where their robust construction is advantageous.</p><p>Another trend is the growing demand for agricultural tires. The agricultural sector heavily relies on diagonal tires due to their ability to provide excellent traction in muddy and uneven terrains. With the expansion of the agricultural industry and the need for efficient farming equipment, the demand for diagonal tires in this sector is expected to witness remarkable growth.</p><p>In conclusion, the diagonal tire market shows promising growth prospects with a projected CAGR of 14.6% during the forecast period. Despite the increasing preference for radial tires in some applications, diagonal tires continue to be popular due to their strength, affordability, and suitability for off-road and agricultural use.</p></p>

<p><p>The diagonal tire market consists of two types: common bias tyre and bias belted tire. Common bias tyres are constructed with layers of fabric cords arranged diagonally across the tire at an angle, which provides good traction and stability. Bias belted tires, on the other hand, have an additional layer of belts made of steel or synthetic material to improve strength and durability. Both types are popular in certain applications where cost-effectiveness and performance in off-road conditions are important.</p></p>

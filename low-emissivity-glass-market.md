<p><p>Low emissivity (Low-E) glass is a type of glass that has a thin coating that helps to reduce the amount of infrared and ultraviolet light that can pass through it. Low-E glass is commonly used in residential and commercial buildings to improve energy efficiency and reduce heating and cooling costs.</p><p>The current outlook for the low emissivity glass market is positive and is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>The low emissivity (LOW-E) glass market comprises three main types: single LOW-E glass, double LOW-E glass, and triple LOW-E glass. Single LOW-E glass is coated with a thin layer of metallic coating, reducing heat transfer. Double LOW-E glass has two layers of metallic coating for enhanced energy efficiency. Triple LOW-E glass offers the highest energy efficiency with three layers of metallic coating. These types of LOW-E glass are widely used in windows and doors to improve thermal insulation, reduce energy consumption, and enhance overall comfort in residential and commercial buildings.</p></p>

<p><p>Neurosurgery Electrosurgical Units (ESUs) are medical devices used in neurosurgical procedures. There are three types of ESUs: Monopole Electrosurgical Units, Bipolar Electrosurgical Units, and Vessel Sealing Units. Monopole Electrosurgical Units use a single electrode for cutting, coagulation, and tissue ablation. Bipolar Electrosurgical Units use two electrodes to coagulate tissues without damaging surrounding areas. Vessel Sealing Units, as the name suggests, are specialized devices used for sealing blood vessels during surgery. These ESUs play a crucial role in neurosurgery by providing precision and control during surgical procedures.</p></p>

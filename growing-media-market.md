<p><p>The growing media market encompasses various types such as soilless mixes, composts, gravel, inert media, and others. Soilless mixes are typically made of organic materials like peat moss, coco coir, and perlite, which provide a well-drained and nutrient-rich environment for plant growth. Composts are organic materials (e.g., decomposed plant matter) that enhance soil fertility and structure. Gravel is a non-degradable material often used in hydroponic systems, providing support to plants while allowing access to water and nutrients. Inert media like rockwool or sand are commonly used in hydroponics as a substrate. Other growing media may include materials like vermiculite, sawdust, or wood chips, which can serve specific plant requirements or cultivation techniques.</p></p>

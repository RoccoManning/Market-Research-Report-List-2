<p><p>The post etch residue removal market includes two main types: aqueous and semi-aqueous. </p><p>Aqueous post etch residue removal refers to the use of a water-based solution to eliminate residues left after the etching process. This method is widely used due to its cost-effectiveness and environmental friendliness.</p><p>On the other hand, semi-aqueous post etch residue removal involves a mixture of water and organic solvents. This method provides more aggressive cleaning power than the aqueous method and is preferred for removing stubborn or complex residues.</p><p>Both aqueous and semi-aqueous methods have their own advantages and are chosen based on the specific needs of the manufacturing process.</p></p>

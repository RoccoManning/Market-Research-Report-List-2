<p><p>Bio-based polypropylene (PP) is a sustainable alternative to conventional polypropylene derived from fossil fuels. It can be produced using different types of biomasses such as sugars, starch, and lignocellulosic biomass. Sugars, obtained from sources like corn or sugarcane, can be fermented to produce bio-based PP. Starch, obtained from crops like potatoes or corn, can be converted into bio-based PP through a process called extrusion. Lignocellulosic biomass, including agricultural residues or wood chips, can also be used to produce bio-based PP through advanced technologies like enzymatic hydrolysis and fermentation.</p></p>

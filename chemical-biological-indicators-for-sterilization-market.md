<p><p>Chemical biological indicators for sterilization refer to two types of products used in assessing the effectiveness of sterilization processes. Biological indicators contain living organisms, such as spores, which are highly resistant to sterilization methods. These indicators are used to confirm that sterilization has successfully killed these organisms. On the other hand, chemical indicators are chemical strips or tapes that change color when exposed to specific conditions, like high temperature or chemical agents, indicating that sterilization has occurred. Both types of indicators play a crucial role in ensuring the safety and efficacy of sterilization processes.</p></p>

<p><p>Non-halogen flame retardants are chemical compounds used to prevent or slow down the spread of fire in various materials. Aluminum hydroxide is commonly used in plastics, coatings, and textiles due to its ability to release water vapor when exposed to high temperatures. Other non-halogen flame retardants include boron compounds, nitrogen-containing compounds, and intumescent agents, which expand when heated to protect the substrate.</p></p>

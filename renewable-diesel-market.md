<p><p>Renewable diesel refers to a sustainable alternative to conventional diesel fuel, derived either from lipids or cellulosic biomass. Lipids are organic compounds found in plants or animals, such as vegetable oils or animal fats, which can be converted into renewable diesel through a process called hydrotreating. On the other hand, cellulosic biomass, which includes agricultural waste, wood residues, or dedicated energy crops, can undergo enzymatic or thermochemical processes to produce renewable diesel. Both market types offer environmentally friendly fuel solutions that significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions compared to traditional diesel fuels.</p></p>

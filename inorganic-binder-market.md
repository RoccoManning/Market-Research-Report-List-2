<p><p>The inorganic binder market can be categorized into different types, namely silicate, phosphate, oxides, sulfate, and borates. Silicate binders, such as sodium silicate, are commonly used for adhesives and coatings due to their high stability and temperature resistance. Phosphate binders, like sodium or potassium phosphates, are widely utilized in the construction industry for cement production. Oxide binders, such as calcium oxide, find application in the iron and steel industry. Sulfate binders, such as calcium sulfate, are commonly utilized in the production of gypsum products. Borates, such as borax, are often found in wood preservation and flame-retardant applications. These varied binder types cater to different industries and applications based on their unique properties and characteristics.</p></p>

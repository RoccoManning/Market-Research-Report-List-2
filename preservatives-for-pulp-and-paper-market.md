<p><strong>The Preservatives for Pulp and Paper Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Oxidizing</li><li>Non-Oxidizing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Preservatives for the pulp and paper market can be categorized into two types: oxidizing and non-oxidizing preservatives. </p><p>Oxidizing preservatives react with the microbial cells by releasing oxygen, which disrupts their metabolism and leads to cell death. These preservatives are effective against a wide range of microorganisms and are particularly useful for water-based systems in the pulp and paper industry.</p><p>On the other hand, non-oxidizing preservatives prevent microbial growth through mechanisms such as inhibiting enzyme systems or disrupting cell membranes. They are typically used for dry applications or where oxidation is undesirable, such as in printing and packaging materials.</p><p>Both oxidizing and non-oxidizing preservatives play a crucial role in maintaining the quality and shelf life of pulp and paper products by preventing the growth of harmful microorganisms.</p></p>

<p><p>As2O3, also known as arsenic trioxide, is an inorganic compound consisting of two atoms of arsenic and three atoms of oxygen. It is commonly found in nature as a mineral called arsenolite. Arsenic trioxide has demonstrated potential in the treatment of certain types of cancer, such as acute promyelocytic leukemia (APL). The pharmaceutical industry's continuous research and development efforts for developing new and improved cancer treatments are expected to create lucrative opportunities for the As2O3 market.</p><p>Moreover, the increasing use of As2O3 in electronics and glass manufacturing sectors for the production of semiconductors, optical fibers, and glass products further fuels market growth.

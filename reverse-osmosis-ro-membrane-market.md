<p><ul><li>Composite Membranes</li><li>Asymmetric Membranes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Reverse Osmosis (RO) Membrane market consists of two types of membranes: Composite Membranes and Asymmetric Membranes. Composite membranes are made up of multiple layers and have a dense, thin skin layer for efficient filtration. They are widely used in water purification systems due to their high rejection rates and durability. Asymmetric membranes, on the other hand, have uneven pore structures that provide better permeability while maintaining high rejection rates. They are mainly used in applications where a high flux is required, such as wastewater treatment and desalination processes.</p></p>

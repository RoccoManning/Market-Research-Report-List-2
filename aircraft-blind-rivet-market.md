<p><p>The Aircraft Blind Rivet Market is classified into two types: With Bucking Bars and Without Bucking Bars. With Bucking Bars refers to the rivet installation process that requires an extra tool called a bucking bar. It involves one person operating the riveting gun and another using the bucking bar to support the rivet's tail while the gun hammers the head. Without Bucking Bars, on the other hand, is a riveting technique that eliminates the need for a bucking bar and can be performed by a single person, resulting in more efficient and cost-effective aircraft assembly.</p></p>

<p><p>Automotive Collision Avoidance Systems encompass several types: Radar, Lidar, Camera, and Ultrasonic. Radar systems utilize radio waves to detect obstacles and calculate their distances, often used for adaptive cruise control. Lidar systems employ laser pulses to measure distances, providing precise object detection and mapping capabilities. Camera systems use image sensors and computer vision algorithms to identify objects and detect potential collisions. Ultrasonic systems use sound waves to determine proximity, commonly employed for parking assistance. Each system plays a critical role in enhancing the safety and effectiveness of collision avoidance technologies in automobiles.</p></p>

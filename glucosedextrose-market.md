<p><p>The glucose (dextrose) market consists of two main types: D-glucose monohydrate and anhydrous glucose. D-glucose monohydrate refers to glucose with one molecule of water attached, while anhydrous glucose refers to glucose without any water molecules. Both types are widely used in various industries such as food and beverages, pharmaceuticals, and cosmetics. While D-glucose monohydrate is preferred for its stability and solubility, anhydrous glucose offers advantages in terms of shelf life and moisture content. Both types play a crucial role as sweeteners, energy sources, and raw materials in these industries.</p></p>

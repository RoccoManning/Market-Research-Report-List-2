<p><p>Medical 3D bioprinting refers to the process of creating three-dimensional structures using living cells in the medical field. Several types of bioprinting techniques are used, including Stereolithography (SLA), Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM), Laminated Object Manufacturing (LOM), and others. SLA involves curing layers of a liquid resin using ultraviolet light to create the structure. FDM utilizes the extrusion of bioinks through a nozzle to build the desired structure. LOM involves the cutting and layering of sheets of material. Other techniques may include inkjet-based or laser-based bioprinting, all aiming to revolutionize tissue engineering and regenerative medicine.</p></p>

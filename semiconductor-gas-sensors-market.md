<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Type I semiconductor gas sensors are based on metal oxide semiconductors, such as tin dioxide or tungsten oxide, and they function by detecting changes in resistance when exposed to certain gases. These sensors are cost-effective, highly sensitive, and widely used in automotive, industrial, and residential applications.</p><p>Type II semiconductor gas sensors are based on metal oxide catalysts, such as platinum or palladium, and they operate on the principle of catalytic combustion. They are mainly used to measure combustible gases and are highly reliable, but comparatively more expensive than Type I sensors.</p></p>
